The light, moist muffins are well-loved by kids and adults alike. 1/2x 1x 2x 3x Ingredients ▢ 4 Tablespoon ( 56 g ) unsalted butter , melted and cooled slightly ▢ 1 ( 1 ) egg , room temperature ▢ ½ teaspoon ( 0.49 tsp ) vanilla extract ▢ ¼ cup ( 60 g ) brown sugar ▢ ¼ cup ( 50 g ) granulated sugar ▢ 1 cup ( 125 g ) all-purpose flour ▢ ½ teaspoon ( 0.49 tsp ) baking powder ▢ ½ teaspoon ( 0.49 tsp ) baking soda ▢ ¼ teaspoon ( 0.25 tsp ) fine sea salt ▢ ⅛ teaspoon ( 0.12 tsp ) cinnamon ▢ ¾ cup ( 200 g ) mashed bananas (~2 medium) , extra ripe ▢ ½ cup ( 94 g ) chocolate chips* Equipment ▢ Muffin Tin ▢ #40 Cookie Scoop Instructions Preheat oven to 350°F and line 8-9 muffin tins with paper liners. In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together melted butter, egg, vanilla extract, and sugars. 4 Tablespoon unsalted butter, 1 egg, ½ teaspoon vanilla extract, ¼ cup brown sugar, ¼ cup granulated sugar Add the flour, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and cinnamon and stir until just combined (the dough will seem dry here – don't worry!). 1 cup all-purpose flour, ½ teaspoon baking powder, ½ teaspoon baking soda, ¼ teaspoon fine sea salt, ⅛ teaspoon cinnamon Fold in mashed bananas and chocolate chips just until incorporated (you don't want to overmix). ¾ cup mashed bananas (~2 medium), ½ cup chocolate chips* Fill muffin liners almost to the top (see note to fill less). If desired, place several additional chocolate chips on top of the muffins. Bake for 20-22 minutes until edges are golden brown and centers bounce back when lightly touched. Remove from pan and let cool slightly before eating. Enjoy! Notes Freeze muffins by wrapping them in plastic wrap and transferring to a freezer bag, or placing them in an airtight container. Thaw in the microwave or at room temperature. The batter will fill 8 muffin tins almost all the way, resulting in larger muffins. Alternatively, fill the muffins 3/4ths of the way full to make 9 muffins with a more domed shape after baking.
